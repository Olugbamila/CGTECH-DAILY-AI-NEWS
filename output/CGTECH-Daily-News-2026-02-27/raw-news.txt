ARTICLE 2: Revolutionizing Big Qualitative Data Analysis: Machine-Assisted Analysis in Healthcare
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0000576

SUMMARY:
A recent study compared human vs. machine-assisted analysis for Big Qualitative Data Analysis, finding that machine-assisted analysis significantly reduced analysis time while requiring human input for expertise and nuance. The study utilized AI tools to analyze a large national survey on peri-operative cardiac arrest, identifying insights to inform targeted interventions and quality improvement initiatives. The approach presents a new method for analyzing large qualitative datasets in public health using AI. The use of AI in health research has grown, but analysis of Big Qualitative Data is a relatively new area of research. The study's findings highlight the potential of AI to enhance patient safety.

Source: Reuters
URL: https://www.reuters.com/commentary/breakingviews/global-markets-breakingviews-2026-02-24/

SUMMARY:
The stock market is experiencing a downturn due to concerns over the impact of artificial intelligence on the job market, with sectors that rapidly adopted machine learning accounting for a third of unemployment since 2022. However, this pessimism may be overstated, and slow monetary and fiscal policy could exacerbate short-term pain. A 'policy bazooka' could potentially mitigate the effects of an AI-driven jobless world. The concept of the 'lump of labour fallacy' suggests that new technologies can create new job opportunities, but the current situation is complex. The key to navigating this challenge lies in finding a balance between embracing AI's benefits and addressing its negative consequences.
