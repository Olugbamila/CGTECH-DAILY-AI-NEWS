ARTICLE 2: MERWACS: The AI-Powered Tool Revolutionizing Kidney Health
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001486

SUMMARY:
Researchers have developed a non-invasive machine learning tool called MERWACS to identify individuals at risk of chronic kidney disease (CKD) without requiring an initial lab test. The tool uses artificial intelligence to analyze easily measured factors such as age, weight, blood pressure, and self-reported medical history. MERWACS generates a personalized predicted probability of having reduced kidney function and has been rigorously tested on large datasets from the US and South Korea. The goal of MERWACS is to provide an early warning to identify which adults and elderly individuals should undergo CKD screening. The tool is available as a free, interactive online application.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine a simple, non-invasive tool that can identify your risk of kidney disease. Meet MERWACS, a machine learning tool that uses AI to analyze your health data and provide a personalized risk assessment.

ARTICLE 4: Large Language Models: The Future of Infectious Disease Forecasting
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001527

SUMMARY:
A recent study published in PLOS Digital Health explores the potential of large language models as predictive engines for forecasting notifiable infectious diseases. The research evaluates the performance of a fine-tuned large language model using public surveillance data from China and the United States, covering 79 infectious diseases. The results show that the large language model is competitive with classical statistical and machine-learning models, particularly for Zoonotic Infectious Diseases and Intestinal Infectious Diseases. This study provides evidence for the use of artificial intelligence in public health forecasting systems. The findings suggest that large language models can be a valuable tool for infectious disease surveillance and epidemic control.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine having a powerful tool to predict infectious disease outbreaks. A new study shows that large language models can be used to forecast notifiable infectious diseases, helping public health officials prepare and respond to epidemics.

ARTICLE 5: Revolutionizing Cancer Patient Care: How Large Language Models Can Improve Outcomes
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001546

SUMMARY:
A recent study published in PLOS Digital Health explores the potential of large language models (LLMs) to extract prognostic information from clinical notes, improving machine learning-based survival prediction and patient stratification for cancer patients. The study analyzed medical records of over 3,500 patients with lung and colon cancer, extracting patient characteristics such as mobility impairment, pain, and comorbidities. The LLM-extracted features substantially improved survival prediction and patient stratification beyond conventional measures. This breakthrough demonstrates the potential of artificial intelligence to unlock prognostic information from clinical records, supporting more informed and personalized clinical decision-making. The study's findings have significant implications for the use of AI in healthcare.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine if doctors could predict cancer patient outcomes more accurately. A new study shows how large language models can extract valuable information from clinical notes, improving survival predictions and patient care.
