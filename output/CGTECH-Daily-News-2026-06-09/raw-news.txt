ARTICLE 3: AI-Powered Snake Identification: A Breakthrough in Healthcare
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/plosntds/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pntd.0014147

SUMMARY:
Researchers have developed a deep learning-based framework for classifying Indian venomous snakes using explainable artificial intelligence. This system can help primary and emergency care providers identify venomous snakes quickly and accurately, improving treatment outcomes. The model was trained on real-world photographs taken in emergency situations, making it more relevant to practical healthcare settings. The system achieved high accuracy and was strengthened by visual interpretability tools and expert verification. This innovation has the potential to improve early triage, rational antivenom use, and surveillance in snakebite-endemic regions.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine being able to identify a venomous snake in seconds, just by taking a photo. Researchers have developed an AI-powered system that can do just that, helping medical professionals provide faster and more effective treatment.
