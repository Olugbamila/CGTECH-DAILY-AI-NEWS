ARTICLE 1: Large Language Models: The Future of Infectious Disease Forecasting?
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001527

SUMMARY:
A recent study published in PLOS Digital Health found that large language models can be effective predictive engines for forecasting notifiable infectious diseases. The models were fine-tuned using public surveillance data from China and the United States, covering 79 infectious diseases. The results showed that large language models performed competitively with classical statistical and machine-learning models, particularly for Zoonotic Infectious Diseases and Intestinal Infectious Diseases. This suggests that fine-tuned large language models can be a valuable tool for infectious disease surveillance and public health forecasting. The study provides a methodological foundation for future AI-driven public health forecasting systems.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine being able to predict infectious disease outbreaks before they happen. A new study shows that large language models can do just that, using public surveillance data to forecast notifiable infectious diseases.

ARTICLE 2: Revolutionizing Cancer Care: How Large Language Models Can Improve Patient Outcomes
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001546

SUMMARY:
A recent study published in PLOS Digital Health has demonstrated the potential of large language models (LLMs) to extract prognostic information from unstructured clinical notes, improving machine learning-based survival prediction and patient stratification for cancer patients. The study applied LLMs to the medical records of over 3,500 patients with lung and colon cancer, extracting patient characteristics such as mobility impairment, pain, and comorbidities. These features were validated against structured EHR data and expert physician annotations, and substantially improved survival prediction and patient stratification beyond conventional measures. This breakthrough has significant implications for personalized clinical decision-making. The use of LLMs could unlock prognostic information from clinical records at scale, supporting more informed treatment plans.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine if doctors could use AI to uncover hidden patterns in patient records, leading to more accurate predictions and personalized care. A new study shows how large language models can extract vital information from clinical notes, improving cancer patient outcomes.
