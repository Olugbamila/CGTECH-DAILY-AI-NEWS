ARTICLE 2: MERWACS: The AI-Powered Tool Revolutionizing Kidney Health
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001486

SUMMARY:
Researchers have developed a non-invasive machine learning tool called MERWACS to identify individuals at risk of chronic kidney disease (CKD). The tool uses artificial intelligence to analyze easily measured factors such as age, weight, blood pressure, and medical history. MERWACS provides a personalized predicted probability of having reduced kidney function and has been rigorously tested on large datasets from the US and South Korea. The goal of MERWACS is to provide an early warning system to identify individuals who should undergo CKD screening, prompting earlier conversations about kidney health. The tool is available as a free, interactive online application.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine a simple tool that can help identify if you're at risk of kidney disease, without needing a lab test. Meet MERWACS, a non-invasive machine learning tool that uses AI to analyze your health info and provide a personalized risk score.

ARTICLE 4: Large Language Models: The Future of Infectious Disease Forecasting?
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001527

SUMMARY:
A recent study published in PLOS Digital Health explores the potential of large language models as predictive engines for forecasting notifiable infectious diseases. The research evaluated a fine-tuned large language model using public surveillance data from China and the United States, covering 79 infectious diseases. The model showed competitive performance compared to classical statistical and machine-learning models, particularly for Zoonotic Infectious Diseases and Intestinal Infectious Diseases. This study provides evidence for the use of large language models in public health forecasting systems. The findings highlight the potential of AI-driven predictive engines in informing timely public health interventions and epidemic control.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine being able to predict infectious disease outbreaks before they happen. A new study shows that large language models can be used as predictive engines to forecast notifiable infectious diseases, outperforming traditional models in some cases.

ARTICLE 5: Revolutionizing Cancer Care: How Large Language Models Are Improving Patient Outcomes
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/digitalhealth/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pdig.0001546

SUMMARY:
Researchers have used large language models to extract prognostic information from clinical notes, improving machine learning-based survival prediction and patient stratification for cancer patients. The study analyzed medical records of over 3,500 patients with lung and colon cancer, extracting features such as mobility impairment, pain, and comorbidities. These features were validated against structured EHR data and expert physician annotations, demonstrating the potential of AI to unlock prognostic information from clinical records. The findings support more informed and personalized clinical decision-making. This breakthrough highlights the potential of large language models in healthcare.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine being able to unlock life-saving information from patient records. Researchers just did that using large language models, improving cancer survival predictions and patient care.
