ARTICLE 3: AI-Powered Snake Identification: A Game-Changer for Global Health
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/plosntds/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pntd.0014147

SUMMARY:
A deep learning-based framework has been developed to classify Indian venomous snakes using images, with the goal of improving primary and emergency care in rural areas. The system uses real-world photographs taken in emergency situations and has achieved high accuracy. The model is strengthened by visual interpretability tools and expert verification to ensure reliability. This AI-assisted classification system has the potential to improve early triage, rational antivenom use, and surveillance in snakebite-endemic regions. By combining AI with human oversight, this work provides a scalable decision-support tool for healthcare providers.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Imagine being able to identify a venomous snake with just a photo. A new AI system can do just that, helping healthcare providers in rural areas make life-saving decisions.
