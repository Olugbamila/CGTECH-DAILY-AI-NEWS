Source: Medical Xpress

ARTICLE 4: PumpKin: A Machine-Learning Pipeline for Tracking Localized Kinematics in C. elegans
Source: PLOS (Public Library of Science)
URL: https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pcbi.1014489

SUMMARY:
Researchers have developed PumpKin, a machine-learning pipeline for tracking localized kinematics in freely moving C. elegans, a microscopic nematode worm. The pipeline is designed to measure the pumping rate of the worm's pharyngeal organ, which is less than 30 μm wide and moves at a speed of 5 pumps per second. This technology has the potential to advance our understanding of behavioral patterns and decision-making in neuroscience. PumpKin is an open-source package that can reliably measure the pumping rate of C. elegans, even in the presence of noise. The development of PumpKin demonstrates the power of AI in enabling the detailed study of complex biological behaviors.

SHORT-FORM SCRIPT:
Meet PumpKin, a machine-learning pipeline that tracks the tiny movements of C. elegans worms. With a width of less than 30 micrometers, these movements are incredibly small, but crucial for understanding behavioral patterns.
